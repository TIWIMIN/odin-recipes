Kenji's Chocolate Chip Cookies

This is a chocolate chip cookie recipe from Serious Eats and J Kenji Lopex Alt. It's main distinctions from other chocolate chip cookie recipes is it's use of solid brown butter, whipped eggs, and sugar blends. It's quite delicious, and it is certainly my favorite chocolate chip cookie I have ever eaten.

Ingredients

  • 225 g unsalted butter
  • 1 ice cube or 30mL frozen water
  • 280g AP flour
  • 3/4 teaspoon (3g) baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons (4g) DC kosher salt
  • 140g granulated sugar
  • 2 (100g) large eggs
  • 2 teaspoon (10ml) vanilla extract
  • 140g dark brown sugar
  • 225g semisweet chocolate
  • sea salt

    • Steps

    1. Brown butter, and then remove from heat. Add ice cube, and then chill in freezer
    2. Thoroughly combine flour, baking soda, and salt. Then whip sugar, eggs, and vanilla until thick.
    3. Combiner brown butter and brown sugar with wet mixture. Then combine wet mixture with dry mixture. Then add chopped chocolate. Do not overmix. Set aside to chill.
    4. Bake at 325F for 13-16 minutes.
    5. Let cookies cool.
    6. When cooled, optionally store in an airtight container for up to 5 days.
