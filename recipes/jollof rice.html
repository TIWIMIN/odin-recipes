NYT's Jollof Rice

This is the New York Time's Jollof Rice recipe. It was developed by Yewande Komolafe. It was one of the first rice dishes I ever made when I first began cooking. To this day, it remains one of my favorites.

Ingredients

Obe Ata

  • 1 (14 oz) can whole peeled tomatoes with juice
  • 1 medium red bell pepper
  • 1/2 medium red onion
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1-inch knob ginger
  • 1 habenero
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil

Jollof Rice

  • 1/2 cup neutral oil
  • 2 medium red onions
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 3 cups parboild jasmine long-grain rice
  • 5 fresh thyme sprigs
  • 1 fresh bay leaf
  • 2 cups stock of choice

    • Steps

    1. Blend obe ata ingredients, except the oil, until smooth.
    2. Add oil to heat and sear the sauce. Reduce until 1/3 of original volume
    3. Preheat oven to 350F. Lightly brown onions with oil. Transfer onions out, and saute garlic. Bloom tomato paste, turmeric, and paprika untill paste darkens.
    4. Add obe ata, rice, thyme, bayleaf, and stock. Cook in oven for 35 minutes.
    5. Remove pot from oven, and let it rest for 15 minutes. Fluff the rice and serve.
