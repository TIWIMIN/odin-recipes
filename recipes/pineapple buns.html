Yi Reservation's Pineapple Buns

This is Yi Reservation's pineapple buns. The recipe has been heavily modified as I've adjusted it for my own needs. This recipe holds a dear place in my heart as it was used to great success for the UCSB TASA Spring Nightmarket.

Ingredients

Bun

  • 310g bread flour
  • 95g TangZhong
  • 1 (60g) large egg
  • 5g yeast
  • 70g heavy cream
  • 50g sugar
  • 3g salt
  • 30g butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 100g milk

TangZhong

  • 20g flour
  • 80g milk

Cookie Crust

  • 110g cake flour
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 50g butter
  • 90g sugar
  • 2g salt
  • 10g milk powder
  • 10g milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/3 teaspoon baking soda

Egg Wash

  • 1 egg yolk
  • 10 g milk

Steps

TangZhong

  1. Combine flour and milk over medium heat. Stir until starch gelatinizes and mixture becomes pasty.

Bun

  1. Combine flour, egg, milk, cream, and let autolyze for 20 minutes
  2. Add salt, yeast, tangzhong, sugar, and knead of medium low for 15 minutes. Then, let dough rest for 5 minutes.
  3. If dough begins to window pane, add butter and vanilla extract. Knead for additional 5-10 minutes. If dough is not at all strong, keep kneading before adding butter.
  4. Let dough cold ferment overnight
  5. Divide dough into 8-10 equal portions, and shape into taut ball.

Cookie Crust

  1. Combine flour, salt, baking soda, baking powder, and milk powder into separate bowl.
  2. Whip butter, egg yolk, milk, and sugar until light and airy.
  3. Combine wet and dry mixture, and be careful not to overmix.
  4. Use plastic wrap to roll into tight log. Let dough rest in fridge overnight
  5. Cut dough into 8-10 equal portions

Final Steps

  • Preheat oven to 365, and rest dough balls until doubled in size. This will be roughly 1 and 1/2 hours.
  • Cover dough balls and wrap flattened cookie crust on top of them. Score cookie crust in a cross hatch.
  • Egg wash the buns, and leave in oven for 20 minutes turning half way through
  • Let rest out of oven for 5 minutes.
